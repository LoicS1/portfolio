Intro to Engineering Design

Entrepreneur Project
May 2016, Introduction to Engineering Design (IED)

The objective of this project was simple- to design a completly new product or a major innovation. My team decided to try to solve a well known problem- lack of clean, drinkable water in third-world countries. We researched three previous solutions: the solar still, rain/dew collector, and atmospheric water generator (AWG). Afterwards, my teammate and I in IED modeled the solution in AutoDesk Inventor, and the two teammates in CSP designed a Cloud9 website to highlight the product. Our product is named Aquaharvester and is a combination of the solar still, rain/dew collector, and AWG with some substantial innovations added. It is portable, with a wooden frame mounted on four large, rubber wheels. The solar still is made of solid, durable plastic, and features an adjustable height using pegs. The rain/dew collector were improved by using lightweight sheet metal instead of plastic, and the addition of a sealed ice container in the center of the metal. We overcame our greatest challenge, cost, by implementing solar panels and recycled materials such as a computer fan, water pump, and plastic tubing. I managed the product documentation, presentation, and team collaboration. I also designed the improved frame, AWG fan and rain collector in AutoDesk Inventor. I learned how to combine mechanical and environmental engineering skills by minimizing energy and material use within the project design, while increasing water output. Our project was successful and earned an A based on the criteria on the rubric.

Project 4.4 Miniature Train
March 2016, Introduction to Engineering Design (IED)

The goal of this project was to design a virtual toy train that can be assembled and programmed to run on tracks, in order to boost a toy train company's sales by targeting today's digitized children. The train needed to be interactive to play with and include features that catered to small children. For our project, my group decided to design a classic locomotive with a control car, dining car, and entertainment car. Each car had special features that were constrained into the final assembly, such as a bell on the entertainment car. Throughout the project, I was responsible for designing the entertainment car as collaborating with my team to make the project presentation . Additionally, I helped make a video of the train running on the tracks and wrote an abstract review after the project was completed. Through this project, I developed my understanding of advanced assembly features in Autodesk Inventor, as well as presentation features within the software. Furthermore, I refined my ability to write a clear and concise design brief.
