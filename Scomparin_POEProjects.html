Entrepreneur Project
May 2017, Principles of Engineering Design

The goal of this four- person project was to design a solution to any real-world problem. My team decided to build a physical prototype, using VEX parts, of a machine that would remove unwanted sediment in reservoirs. We had to use lightweight yet strong material, such as steel and titanium for the frame, while designing a net system to pick up sediment, which was the most cost efficient option system available.

Our final solution consisted of a submarine with a rigid, metal exterior frame and central cavity, equipped at the front with wire mesh and light sensor at the front opening, and a fine net and metal plates on the rear opening. The submarine would patrol the reservoir using its mechanical propellers, and begin filtering sediment in the back net when the light sensor detected the water was murky and full of suspended sediment.

As project leader, I was responsible for managing most of the project documentation, part of the presentation, communicating with my teammates regarding the project design and timeline, and designing the submarine's exterior frame. I learned a lot in my field of interest, environmental engineering, by analyzing how different designs would respond to a complex environmental problem (sediment accumulation). However, this project also introduced me to a multi-disciplinary problem-solving approach, as I was also exposed to the programming of the sensor and testing of the submarine's mechanical structure.
